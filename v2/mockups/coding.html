Interviewer: Can you tell me about your experience working from home during the past year?

P08: Sure. It's been quite a journey, honestly. At first, I was excited about the flexibility. I could finally have breakfast with my family and skip the long commute. That alone was a huge improvement for my quality of life.

Interviewer: That sounds positive. Were there any challenges?

P08: Oh, definitely. The biggest challenge is that we can't just walk over to someone's desk anymore. Messages get lost in Slack, and sometimes you wait hours for a response that would have taken 30 seconds in person. It really slows things down.

And then there's the meeting fatigue. We have back-to-back video calls all day. By 3pm, I'm completely drained. My eyes hurt, my back hurts from sitting, and I feel like I haven't accomplished anything meaningful.

Interviewer: How do you manage the work-life balance?

P08: That's the tricky part. When your home is your office, it's hard to disconnect. I find myself checking emails at 9pm, or starting work at 7am because the laptop is right there. My partner has started hiding my laptop after dinner just to force me to stop.

But I've also learned to set better boundaries. I now have a dedicated workspace, and when I leave that room, work is done. I've also blocked my calendar from 12-1pm for lunch - no meetings allowed.

Interviewer: That's a great strategy. What about team collaboration?

P08: We've had to completely reinvent how we work together. The spontaneous hallway conversations where great ideas were born - those don't happen anymore. We've tried virtual coffee chats, but it's not the same.
